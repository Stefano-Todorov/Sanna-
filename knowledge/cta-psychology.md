# CTA Psychology — Why Each Call-to-Action Type Works

Every CTA leverages a psychological principle. Match the principle to the content type for maximum conversion.

## CTA Types & Psychology

### 1. Follow for More (Reciprocity + Anticipation)
**Psychology:** You just gave them free value. Reciprocity makes them feel they "owe" something back. Anticipation of more value seals it.
**When to use:** After delivering genuine, specific value — tips, tutorials, insights
**Formula:** "If this helped, follow for [specific promise of future value]"
**Examples:**
- "I share stuff like this every day — follow so you don't miss the next one"
- "Follow me for more [niche] breakdowns like this"
**Do NOT use after:** Entertainment-only content, vague content, content that didn't deliver value

### 2. Comment Below (Social Validation + Identity)
**Psychology:** People comment when they can signal identity ("I'm the type who..."), share opinions, or answer easy questions. Comments also create community belonging.
**When to use:** After opinion/debate content, relatable content, or content with a natural question
**Formula:** "Comment [specific thing]" — the more specific the prompt, the higher the response rate
**Examples:**
- "Which one are you — type A or type B? Comment below"
- "Drop a [emoji] if this has happened to you"
- "What's your biggest struggle with X? Tell me in the comments"
**Key insight:** Binary choices ("this or that") get more comments than open questions

### 3. Share This (Social Currency)
**Psychology:** People share content that makes THEM look good — smart, funny, helpful, in-the-know. The share is about the sharer's identity, not your content.
**When to use:** After content that would make someone look good for sharing — useful tips, funny takes, important warnings
**Formula:** "Send this to someone who [specific person/situation]"
**Examples:**
- "Send this to your friend who always does X"
- "Share this with someone who needs to hear it"
- "Tag someone who would find this useful"
**Key insight:** "Send to ONE specific person" outperforms "share with everyone"

### 4. Save for Later (Loss Aversion + Utility)
**Psychology:** Saving is driven by fear of losing access to valuable information. It signals "I might need this" — works best with reference-style content.
**When to use:** After tutorials, lists, step-by-step guides, cheat sheets, anything with reference value
**Formula:** "Save this so you have it when you need it"
**Examples:**
- "Save this for next time you need to X"
- "Bookmark this — you're going to want it later"
- "Save this before it gets buried in your feed"
**Key insight:** "Save" is the highest-quality engagement signal — it indicates genuinely useful content

### 5. Watch Again (Curiosity + Completionism)
**Psychology:** Tells the viewer they missed something, triggering curiosity and the need to verify. Replays are algorithmic gold.
**When to use:** After dense/fast content, content with hidden details, or anything with layers
**Formula:** "Watch again — you missed [specific thing]"
**Examples:**
- "Watch this again — there's a detail you missed the first time"
- "The second watch hits different"
- "Rewatch the beginning knowing what you know now"
**Key insight:** This works best when there genuinely IS something to catch on rewatch

### 6. Link in Bio (Scarcity + Next Step)
**Psychology:** Bridges social content to external conversion. Works when the video created enough desire for a "next step."
**When to use:** When you have something specific to offer — free resource, product, newsletter, full guide
**Formula:** "Link in bio for [specific thing they want]"
**Examples:**
- "I put the full guide in my bio — it's free"
- "Link in bio if you want the template I used"
- "The full breakdown is on my site — link in bio"
**Key insight:** Only works if the video created genuine desire. Never use after weak content.

## CTA Rules

1. **One CTA per video** — multiple CTAs = no action. Pick the ONE thing you want them to do
2. **Match CTA to content type** — a funny meme shouldn't end with "link in bio for my course"
3. **Be specific** — "comment below" < "comment which one you are — type 1 or type 2"
4. **Earn the CTA** — deliver value FIRST, then ask. Reciprocity requires giving before asking
5. **Natural delivery** — the CTA should feel like a continuation, not a commercial break
6. **CTA hierarchy by platform value:**
   - TikTok: Share > Save > Comment > Follow > Like
   - Instagram: Share > Save > Comment > Follow > Like
   - YouTube: Subscribe > Comment > Like > Share
