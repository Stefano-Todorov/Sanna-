# Hook Formulas — Proven Short-Form Video Openers

Use these formulas when writing hooks. Match the formula to the content type and audience.

## The 12 Core Hook Patterns

### 1. Curiosity Gap
Opens a loop the viewer MUST close. Teases an outcome without revealing it.
- "I tried X for 30 days and the results shocked me"
- "There's one thing nobody tells you about X"
- "I found out why X happens and it changes everything"
**Best for:** Transformations, experiments, reveals, myth-busting

### 2. Pattern Interrupt
Says the opposite of what the audience expects. Breaks autopilot scrolling.
- "Stop doing X — it's actually hurting you"
- "Everyone says X but they're wrong"
- "I'm a [professional] and I'd never do X"
**Best for:** Contrarian takes, expert advice, debunking myths

### 3. Direct Challenge
Calls out the viewer's behavior or beliefs. Creates an emotional reaction.
- "You're doing X wrong and here's proof"
- "If you're still doing X in 2024, we need to talk"
- "This is why you're not getting results with X"
**Best for:** Educational content, accountability, tough love

### 4. Storytelling / Mid-Action
Drops into the middle of a story at the most dramatic point.
- "So there I was at 3am when..."
- "I just got fired and here's what I did next"
- "This morning something happened that changed my entire strategy"
**Best for:** Personal stories, case studies, behind-the-scenes

### 5. Social Proof / Authority
Leads with credentials or results to establish instant trust.
- "As someone who's [credential], here's what I know..."
- "I've helped 500+ people do X — here's the pattern"
- "After 10 years in X industry, this is the #1 mistake I see"
**Best for:** Expert positioning, tutorials, advice

### 6. List / Number Promise
Sets a clear expectation of structured, scannable value.
- "3 things that changed my X forever"
- "5 mistakes killing your X right now"
- "The 7 rules I follow to X every single time"
**Best for:** Tips, mistakes, rules, frameworks, how-tos

### 7. Question Hook
Asks something the viewer instinctively wants to answer.
- "What would you do if X happened?"
- "Want to know the fastest way to X?"
- "Have you ever wondered why X happens?"
**Best for:** Relatable content, community engagement, polls

### 8. Negative / Fear-Based
Triggers loss aversion — people fear missing out or making mistakes.
- "Don't do X until you watch this"
- "This one mistake cost me $X"
- "I wish someone told me this before I started X"
**Best for:** Warnings, mistakes content, cautionary tales

### 9. Before/After Tease
Promises a visible transformation without showing the full result yet.
- "Watch how I turned X into Y"
- "This is what X looks like after 90 days"
- "The difference is insane — swipe to see"
**Best for:** Transformations, tutorials, results content

### 10. Bold Statement / Hot Take
Makes an unqualified, provocative claim that demands engagement.
- "X is dead and nobody wants to admit it"
- "This is the best X and it's not even close"
- "Unpopular opinion: X is completely overrated"
**Best for:** Opinion content, trend commentary, debate-starters

### 11. "Secret" / Insider Knowledge
Frames the content as exclusive or hidden information.
- "The algorithm hack nobody's talking about"
- "Here's what X professionals do that you don't"
- "I'm not supposed to share this but..."
**Best for:** Tips, hacks, industry secrets, behind-the-scenes

### 12. Relatability / "POV"
Puts the viewer in a shared experience that triggers recognition.
- "POV: you just realized X"
- "Tell me you're a X without telling me"
- "When X happens and you don't know what to do"
**Best for:** Humor, community building, niche-specific content

## Hook Writing Rules

1. **First 2 words matter most** — they decide if someone keeps reading/listening
2. **Specificity beats vagueness** — "3 things" > "some things", "$47K" > "a lot of money"
3. **One idea per hook** — don't try to set up multiple premises
4. **Match the energy to the content** — a calm tutorial shouldn't open with "YOU WON'T BELIEVE THIS"
5. **Text hooks on screen should be ≤ 8 words** — spoken hooks can be longer
6. **Test the "scroll test"** — if you saw this while scrolling at full speed, would you stop?
7. **Never fully resolve the hook** — the body should deliver, not the opening
