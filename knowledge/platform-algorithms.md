# Platform Algorithm Knowledge — What Each Platform Actually Rewards

Understanding platform mechanics is essential for analyzing WHY content performs and generating content that will perform.

## TikTok

### Primary Ranking Signals (in order of weight)
1. **Watch time / completion rate** — THE most important metric. A 30s video watched to 27s beats a 60s video watched to 40s
2. **Rewatch rate** — loops and replays signal extremely high quality
3. **Share rate** — shares (especially to DMs) are the strongest engagement signal
4. **Comment rate** — comments indicate the content sparked something worth responding to
5. **Save rate** — saves indicate reference-worthy or high-utility content
6. **Like rate** — lowest-weight engagement signal (easy/passive action)

### What This Means for Content
- **Shorter is often better** — a tight 20s video with 95% completion beats a loose 60s with 50% completion
- **Hooks are existential** — first 1-2 seconds determine if TikTok even shows it to more people
- **Loop-friendly endings** — videos that seamlessly loop get replay boosts
- **Shareability > likability** — content people send to friends ("you need to see this") outperforms content people just double-tap
- **Comment bait works** — asking questions, leaving something slightly wrong, hot takes all drive comments
- **Saves = utility** — tutorials, lists, and reference content get saved more

### TikTok-Specific Patterns
- Videos under 30s have highest average completion rates
- Text overlays increase watch time (people pause/rewatch to read)
- Trending sounds give a small initial distribution boost but are NOT required
- Posting time matters less than content quality — the algorithm is patient
- Niche content outperforms broad content for small creators (the algorithm finds your audience)

## Instagram Reels

### Primary Ranking Signals
1. **Watch time + completion** — same as TikTok, this is #1
2. **Shares (Send to)** — Instagram has explicitly said sends are the top engagement signal for Reels
3. **Saves** — Instagram weighs saves heavily as a quality signal
4. **Replays** — rewatching signals high-quality content
5. **Comments** — especially longer comments and replies
6. **Likes** — lower weight but still tracked
7. **Follows from Reel** — signals the content attracted new audience

### What This Means for Content
- **Shares are king on IG** — meme-style, relatable, "tag someone who..." content gets shared
- **Saves = bookmarkable** — carousels disguised as Reels, tip content, reference material
- **IG favors polished over raw** — slightly more produced content tends to perform vs. TikTok's raw aesthetic
- **Caption strategy matters more** — IG users actually read captions; use them for depth
- **Hashtags still work on IG** — 3-5 targeted hashtags outperform 30 generic ones
- **Cover image matters** — IG shows Reels on the grid; an ugly thumbnail = fewer profile-visit plays

### Instagram-Specific Patterns
- Cross-posting from TikTok (with watermark) is penalized
- Original audio tends to be boosted over trending audio
- Reels between 15-30s have highest average completion
- Collaboration Reels get shown to both audiences
- Posting to Feed + Reels (not just Reels tab) increases distribution

## YouTube Shorts

### Primary Ranking Signals
1. **Click-through rate (CTR)** — thumbnail + title matter more than on TikTok/IG
2. **Watch time + completion** — same core metric as all platforms
3. **Swipe-away rate** — if people swipe away quickly, distribution drops fast
4. **Engagement rate** — comments, likes, shares combined
5. **Subscribe from Short** — new subscribers signal high-value content
6. **Traffic to long-form** — YouTube rewards Shorts that funnel viewers to full videos

### What This Means for Content
- **Thumbnail matters most here** — YouTube Shorts have visible thumbnails in some placements
- **CTR optimization** — title should complement the hook, not repeat it
- **YouTube audience skews older** — more polished, informative content often outperforms raw/trendy
- **Funnel to long-form** — end with "full video on my channel" to get algorithmic bonus
- **Educational content dominates** — YouTube's DNA is learning; lean into teaching
- **Longer Shorts perform well** — 45-60s Shorts often outperform 15s on YouTube (audience expects more depth)

### YouTube-Specific Patterns
- Shorts between 30-60s tend to perform best (longer than TikTok/IG sweet spot)
- Keyword-rich titles help discoverability (YouTube is a search engine)
- Consistency in posting time matters more on YouTube
- Series content ("Part 1, 2, 3") drives subscribe behavior
- YouTube explicitly boosts original content over cross-posts

## Cross-Platform Insights

| Factor | TikTok | Instagram | YouTube |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ideal length | 15-30s | 15-30s | 30-60s |
| Top signal | Watch time | Shares | CTR + Watch time |
| Aesthetic | Raw, authentic | Polished but real | Informative, produced |
| Audio | Trending helps | Original preferred | Less important |
| Hashtags | Optional | 3-5 targeted | Keywords in title |
| Best content | Entertainment + education | Relatable + shareable | Educational + series |
| Growth mechanic | FYP algorithm | Explore + Shares | Search + Suggested |
