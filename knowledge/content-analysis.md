# Content Analysis Framework — How to Analyze Why Content Performs

Use this framework when analyzing videos. Move beyond surface-level observations to identify the actual mechanics of performance.

## The 5-Layer Analysis Model

### Layer 1: Hook Mechanics (First 1-3 Seconds)
Analyze the opening and classify it:
- **What hook formula was used?** (Curiosity gap, pattern interrupt, question, etc.)
- **Was the hook text-on-screen, spoken, or both?** Text+spoken > spoken only
- **How many seconds until the premise is clear?** Under 2s = strong, over 3s = at risk
- **Does the hook create an open loop?** (A question/tension that requires watching to resolve)
- **"Scroll test"** — would you stop scrolling at full speed for this?

### Layer 2: Retention Structure (The Body)
Analyze what keeps viewers watching past the hook:
- **What framework is used?** (AIDA, PAS, List, Story, Tutorial — see script-frameworks.md)
- **Is there escalation?** Each section should be more interesting than the last
- **What's the pacing?** New info/visual every 3-5 seconds = good. Stagnant = bad
- **Are there "micro-hooks"?** ("But here's where it gets crazy", "Wait, it gets better")
- **Visual variety:** Jump cuts, text overlays, B-roll, transitions — anything to maintain visual interest
- **Audio engagement:** Music, sound effects, voice modulation — monotone kills retention

### Layer 3: Engagement Triggers (Why People React)
Analyze what drives likes, comments, shares, saves:
- **Shareability:** Would someone send this to a specific friend? What would they say when sending it?
- **Comment triggers:** Does it provoke opinions, identification ("that's me!"), or debate?
- **Save triggers:** Is there reference value — something worth coming back to?
- **Emotional response:** What emotion does it trigger? (surprise, validation, FOMO, aspiration, humor, outrage)
- **Identity signaling:** Does engaging with this content say something about who the viewer is?

### Layer 4: Production & Format
Analyze the technical execution:
- **Format type:** Talking head, voiceover, tutorial, POV, greenscreen, duet/stitch, B-roll montage
- **Edit complexity:** Raw single-take vs. jump cuts vs. full production
- **Text overlays:** What text is on screen? Is it the hook? Subtitles? Key points?
- **Thumbnail/cover:** What does the first frame look like? (Especially important for IG/YT)
- **Audio:** Original vs. trending sound. Music vs. no music. Volume/energy level
- **Duration:** How long? Does the length match the content density?

### Layer 5: Context & Timing
Analyze external factors:
- **Trend alignment:** Is this riding a current trend, sound, or format?
- **Niche positioning:** How specific is the target audience? Narrow niche = more engaged viewers
- **Posting context:** Part of a series? Response to something? Timely topic?
- **Creator's existing audience:** How much of this performance is organic vs. built audience?

## Engagement Ratio Analysis

Use these benchmarks to assess performance:

| Metric | Poor | Average | Good | Exceptional |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Like rate (likes/views) | <2% | 2-5% | 5-10% | >10% |
| Comment rate | <0.1% | 0.1-0.5% | 0.5-2% | >2% |
| Share rate | <0.1% | 0.1-0.3% | 0.3-1% | >1% |
| Save rate | <0.2% | 0.2-1% | 1-3% | >3% |

**Key ratios to watch:**
- High saves, low comments = utility content (tutorials, lists)
- High comments, low saves = opinion/debate content
- High shares = socially valuable content (relatable, funny, important)
- High likes but low everything else = passively consumed, not deeply engaging

## What to Look For in Competitor Analysis

When analyzing a competitor's content patterns across multiple posts:
1. **Recurring hook formulas** — most successful creators rotate 2-3 hook types
2. **Consistent format** — do their top posts share a format? (talking head, list, etc.)
3. **Topic clusters** — what topics consistently hit vs. flop?
4. **Posting cadence** — how often do they post? Is there a pattern to timing?
5. **Evolution** — has their style changed recently? What changed and did metrics follow?
6. **Their 80/20** — which 20% of their content types drive 80% of their views?

## Actionable Insight Requirements

Every piece of analysis should answer:
1. **What specifically worked?** (Not "good hook" but "curiosity gap hook with a specific number that created an open loop")
2. **Why did it work?** (The psychological/algorithmic mechanism)
3. **How can this creator apply it?** (Adapted to their niche, not copied verbatim)
